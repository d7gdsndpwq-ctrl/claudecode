## About

I work in data governance inside a bank's Chief Data Office — master data management, data
quality, and regulatory compliance. Day to day, that means understanding exactly how
sensitive data should and shouldn't move through an organisation, and what it takes to use
new technology without breaking that.

Outside that role, I help accounting, mortgage broking and financial planning firms do the
same thing on a smaller scale. Most firms in this space already have staff using AI tools
informally — pasting client financials, identity documents or case notes into public
chatbots, with no policy, no audit trail, and no one who's thought through what that means
for client confidentiality or regulatory obligations. The productivity upside is real; the
exposure is usually invisible until something goes wrong.

I run a free in-house AI seminar for firms who want to get ahead of this — a practical,
no-pitch session covering where AI genuinely helps in their day-to-day work and where it
creates risk, specific to how their firm actually handles client data. For firms that want
to go further, that leads into a paid AI Readiness & Governance Assessment. Based in the
Hills District, Sydney — happy to talk it through before you commit to anything.
